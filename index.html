Pyramids

The most famous pyramids were constructed in ancient Egypt during the Old Kingdom period (around 2686-2181 BCE).

The Castle

Castles emerged during the Middle Ages in response to the need for protection and defense against invaders.

Cairo Tower and the Nile River

The Cairo Tower, also known as the Cairo TV Tower or the Cairo Sky Tower, is an iconic landmark located in Cairo, the capital city of Egypt.

Alexandria Library

The ancient Library of Alexandria was founded in the 3rd century BCE and served as a major center of learning and scholarship in the ancient world. The library aimed to collect and preserve knowledge from various disciplines.

Alexandria beach

The city has several popular beaches that cater to both locals and tourists. Some of the well-known beaches in Alexandria include: Montaza Beach,Mamoura Beach,Stanley Beach and Abu Qir Beach.

Luxor

Luxor served as the capital of ancient Egypt during the New Kingdom period (1550-1070 BCE) and was known as Thebes. It was a major political and religious center and played a crucial role in ancient Egyptian civilization.

Aswan

Aswan is situated about 690 kilometers (430 miles) south of Cairo. It is located in the Nile Valley and is surrounded by desert landscapes. Aswan is known for its picturesque setting, with the Nile River flowing through the city and several islands dotting its waters.

Taba

Taba is located on the eastern coast of the Sinai Peninsula, on the borders with Palestine and Jordan. It is famous for its beautiful coastal scenery, as it is bordered by the Red Sea to the east and rugged mountains to the west.

Sharm El-Shaikh

Sharm El-Sheikh is situated on the Red Sea coast and is known for its stunning beaches and clear blue waters. It is part of the South Sinai Governorate in Egypt.Sharm El-Sheikh has a desert climate, characterized by hot temperatures, especially during the summer months. Winters are milder and more comfortable, making it a year-round destination.

Siwa

Siwa has a rich ancient history dating back thousands of years. It was once a significant cultural and religious center and is famous for the Oracle of Amun, where people sought guidance and prophecies. Some notable archaeological sites in Siwa include:Temple of Amun,Shali Fortress.Some notable natural attractions include:Great Sand Sea,Salt Lakes and Mountainous Terrain.

New Alamein City

New Alamein City is situated on the northwest coast of Egypt, approximately 84 kilometers west of Alexandria. It is part of the Matrouh Governorate.The city is a flagship project of the Egyptian government aimed at creating a modern, sustainable, and attractive urban center. It is designed to accommodate residential, commercial, and recreational areas to alleviate population pressure on existing cities like Cairo and Alexandria.

WELCOME TO EGYPT

Egypt is a transcontinental country located mainly in the northeastern corner of Africa, with the Sinai Peninsula forming a land bridge to Southwest Asia.Egypt has a rich historical and cultural heritage, with a history dating back to ancient times. The ancient Egyptian civilization, with its pyramids, temples, and pharaohs, is famous worldwide. The Nile River, which flows through Egypt, played a crucial role in the development of its civilization. Egypt has a diverse economy, with sectors such as agriculture, industry, and services contributing to its GDP. Tourism is a significant industry due to Egypt's historical sites, including the Pyramids of Giza, the Sphinx, Luxor, and Aswan.

Why is Cairo a popular tourist destination?

there are numerous activities in Cairo such as exploring all the archaeological attractions such as the great pyramids of Giza, the glorious Cairo citadel, and more. You will get to discover the great Egyptian museum, do some shopping in khan el Khalili, board a dinner cruise, and explore the nightlife, and more.


What is Alexandria famous for?

Alexandria was best known for the Lighthouse of Alexandria (Pharos), one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World; its Great Library, the largest in the ancient world; and the Catacombs of Kom El Shoqafa, one of the Seven Wonders of the Middle Ages.


Why do tourists go to Luxor?

Most people come to Luxor to explore the West Bank but there are several draws on this side of the Nile, too. The highlights are the temples of Luxor and Karnak, which were once joined by a 2km-long avenue of sphinxes, some of which still adorn the entrance today.


Why should I go to Aswan?

Many of the statues, obelisks, and building materials for temples and shrines in Egypt were cut from the quarries in Aswan. In fact, one of the best things to do in Aswan is to visit one of these quarries to see the Unfinished Obelisk. Aswan is one of the sunniest places on Earth.


What is Siwa famous for?

It is famed from its role in ancient Egypt as the home to an oracle of Amun, the ruins of which are a popular tourist attraction, giving it the ancient name Oasis of Amun-Ra, after the major Egyptian deity. Clockwise from top: Shali Mountain village, Ruins of the Old Siwa, Lake Aftnas, Pigeon Towers, oasis near Siwa.


What countries can you see from Taba, Egypt?

Strategically located on the Gulf of Aqaba on the Red Sea, Taba Heights beachfront resort promises stunning views of the Sinai, Egypt's mountains and turquoise waters, while overlooking the borders of Jordan, Palestine and Saudi Arabia – each just a boat ride away.
